NORE

Branding for a rock academy student

challenge

a striking
artist identity

During the first year of my studies in IT & Media Design, I made a complete branding for a rock academy student together with 3 other classmates. This student's name was Tessa Meester, and she was in the music group NORE.

NORE is a Dutch indie pop-rock band. Tessa (the leader of the band) makes a lot of art herself, and she hopes to bring art and music together with NORE. In this case, you'll travel along through 4 design sprints in which we created a unique branding and website for NORE.

branding

hitting the
right chord

Colors
By creating guidelines for NORE's branding, we established key design principles to use in further media products such as the website and photography. To begin with, it was important to create a color palette that fit our client's needs. Our client indicated that they liked the colors blue, purple, red and green. When selecting from these colors, we looked at which ones clash, and which ones provide a nice contrast.

We also looked at the core values that NORE wanted to convey. In particular, the retro-like feel was very important to them. Also the feeling of togetherness and a relaxed feeling was important to them. With these core values in mind, we came up with this color palette together.

Typography
It was important to see what font we were going to use for the website, and in other places like social media posts. The information on the website needs to be easy to read, so we stuck to fonts like Poppins and Montserrat.

Tessa likes to combine her art with music, so we thought it would be fun to use a playful font in some places. We chose to work with Tessa to create a font from her handwriting. This made it completely match her style, and NORE's. The band was very enthusiastic about this.

website

a place to
express

After we were on the same page with NORE regarding the branding, we started translating this into a design for the website using Adobe XD. In doing so, we used not only the color palette and typography, but also NORE's art. We used this to make the site look a little retro and playful.



Tessa also felt it was important for her art to have a special place on the site. Therefore, we used JavaScript to write a content filter. This allows Tessa to upload all of her art to a single webpage, and then the user can select which art is displayed. Super convenient!



Responsive
Many visitors to the website look on mobile devices, so it was important to make everything responsive. Therefore, we replaced the navigation bar at the top of the desktop interface with an expandable hamburger menu.

it's a wrap!
